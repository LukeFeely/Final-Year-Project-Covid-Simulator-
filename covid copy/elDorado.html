Challenge 3 - El Dorado


The country you have been assigned is El Dorado. This will be very similar to the last simulator with some small but very important differences which will impact how the disease spreads.




The first main difference is that people travelling from abroad will now be taken into account.

People will travel into your communities from the outside world or go the opposite direction, depending on how high the External Travel Restrictions are set. These will be seperate to the internal restrictions which you used earlier which will dictate how often people move between communities.



El Dorado's Key Differences:

  • Vaccination Program - El Dorado's vaccination program has been quite slow so you will be limited to only being able to vaccinate one person a day max.

  • Compliance - El Dorado has suffered issues with compliance so your compliance will deteriorate twice as fast as it did in the previous challenge

  • Fatality Rate - El Dorado has a somewhat older population so the fatality rate will be slightly increased as older people are more vulnerable to the disease.

  • Weak Borders - El Dorado has failed to implement successful restrictions for preventing people entering El Dorado from abroad, so the External Travel Restrictions will be fixed quite low.

  • Large Budget - El Dorado has borrowed significantly and spent a lot to attempt to deal with this pandemic so you will be allocated a larger budget of €150,000,000.


Your Challenge:

Your challenge will be to keep the death percentage as low as possible using the allocated €150,000,000 budget. Similar to the last simulation, once you exceed this budget, the variables will return to their initial value and you will have no control. Feel free to have several attempts with different strategies and note how they differed. When you have your paremeters set, click start to begin. The simulation will run until there are no more active infections, or until 100 days have passed.
